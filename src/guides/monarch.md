# Switching from Monarch

Monarch showed you your money. SkrillaPlan asks you to direct it. That's
the real difference, and it's worth understanding before you move a single
transaction.

## Watching isn't steering

Monarch is a tracking app: it gathers your accounts, draws the dashboards,
and tells you what happened. Useful — the way a rearview mirror is useful.

Envelope budgeting works the other way around. You decide what your dollars
do *before* they do it, and the app keeps score against your plan. Watching
becomes steering. If the method is new to you, read
[Envelope budgeting, plainly](/envelope-budgeting/) first — five minutes,
and the rest of this guide makes twice the sense.

## Bring your history

1. **Export from Monarch:** download your transactions as CSV.
2. **Drop the file** on our [Import screen](/screens/import/). If the
   columns need pointing out, a mapping step appears — date, amount,
   description — and a preview shows exactly what will land. Nothing
   imports until you commit.
3. Import twice by accident? Nothing doubles. Duplicates are skipped by
   fingerprint, always.

## Turn categories into envelopes

Your Monarch categories carry over with your transactions — now give them
jobs:

- On the [Categories screen](/screens/categories/), rename what's close,
  archive what isn't you, and shelve related categories into groups.
  Fewer beats more: if two categories are hard to tell apart, they're one.
- Where Monarch had savings goals, set a **goal on a category** — click
  its name on the [Budget screen](/screens/budget/): monthly funding, a
  target balance, or a target by a date. Same intent, but the money for it
  sits in a named envelope instead of a wish.

## Where your dashboards went

[Reports](/screens/reports/) covers the ground: the monthly overview,
cash flow and your savings rate, category trends with six-month
sparklines, spending-changes ("what's different this month"), and the
recurring-subscriptions list with price-increase flags. The difference:
every chart is measured against a plan you set, not just a past you had.

## The privacy difference

Monarch's own privacy policy discloses session-replay tooling and
advertising partners. SkrillaPlan runs zero trackers — no analytics, no
ads, no session recording, nothing third-party at all. Your money is your
business; the full short list is at [What we don't do](/what-we-dont-do/).

## What to expect the first week

The method asks more of you than tracking did — one assignment session on
payday, a few five-minute check-ins. In exchange, the app stops telling
you what happened and starts telling you whether the plan is working.
Most switchers say the same thing after a month: the dashboards were
interesting, but *deciding* is what changed the numbers.

Welcome over. [Getting started](/getting-started/) takes it from here.
