# Transfers and the Special tab

Some money movements lie to budgets. This guide is about how SkrillaPlan
catches the lie — and what to do the few times it needs your help.

## The lie, in one example

You pay your credit card: $1,200. Your checking account shows $1,200 out.
Your card account shows $1,200 in. A naive budget reads that as $1,200 of
spending *and* $1,200 of income — in a month where neither happened. It's
your own money changing pockets.

The real spending already happened, swipe by swipe, and got categorized
transaction by transaction. Counting the payment too would count it twice.

## What SkrillaPlan does about it

It detects the pairs and moves them to the **Special** tab on the
[Transactions screen](/screens/transactions/), out of your spending, income,
and reports. Labeled:

- **💳 Credit-card payment** — checking out, card in.
- **🔁 Transfer** — savings to checking, checking to savings, any pocket to
  any pocket.

Detection works best when it can see both pockets. Connect both sides —
checking *and* the credit card — and payments are caught automatically.
One-sided movements are just harder to prove. ([Banks](/screens/banks/) is
where you connect.)

## When it asks for help

Certainty isn't always available, and we don't guess silently:

- **❓ Possible transfer** — the amounts match but the signal is weak.
  These **still count as spending** until you decide: **Confirm** moves it
  to Special, **Dismiss** leaves it counting. We surface; we don't hide
  your money.
- **Wrong call the other way?** Anything on the Special tab has
  **Not a transfer** — click it and the transaction goes back to counting.

That's the whole maintenance: a glance at the ❓ rows when they appear.

## But I budget for my card payment — where does that live?

Two different things, and the app keeps them straight:

- **Assigning money** for the payment happens on the
  [Budget screen](/screens/budget/) — the card payment's category gets its
  share, like rent gets rent.
- **The payment itself** — the $1,200 moving between accounts — lands in
  Special and stays out of your spending.

The plan is yours. The bookkeeping is handled.

## While you're on the tab

Below the transfers sits **Recurring bills & subscriptions** — everything
that charges you on a schedule, with its typical amount. Worth a slow read
once a month.
