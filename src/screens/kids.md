# The Kids screen

A screen you can hand to a child. It shows them what they have, what's
coming, what they're saving for, and how many weeks until they can buy it.
Nothing else — no accounts, no transactions, no budget.

Find it in the top nav. On a phone, tap **⋯ More**, then **Kids**. A tablet
held sideways is what it's built for.

## There is no child account

No login, no email, no password. A child never signs in to anything.

The only thing stored about a child is a first name you type. No birthdate,
no last name, no photo. Ask what we know about your kid and the answer is:
what you'd call them at dinner.

## The allowance is real money

Set an allowance and it doesn't create a pretend ledger. It creates an actual
transaction, out of an envelope you pick, on the schedule you set. Give $10 a
week and $10 a week genuinely leaves that envelope.

That's the point. The family's books and the child's books are the same
money — so the allowance is budgeted for like everything else, instead of
found in a wallet on Saturday.

Watch the arithmetic, because most of us get it wrong: **$10 a week is about
$43 a month**, not $40. There are more than four weeks in most months. The
app shows you the monthly figure while you're setting it up, for exactly that
reason.

If a device sits unused for a while, nothing is lost — the next payment
catches up what's owed, up to eight periods back.

## Saving up

A child picks something they want and the screen does the waiting math: what
they have, what's coming, how many weeks until it's theirs. That countdown is
the whole lesson, and it teaches itself.

The screen is written for a child — short words, warm, no budgeting talk.
It's their screen, not a small copy of yours.

There are a couple of achievements over there too, theirs to earn.

## Handing over the device

**Hand over this device** on a kid's card starts kid mode. The device then
shows only that child's screen until someone enters the PIN — **Grown-ups**,
then four digits, and you're back.

Kid mode is per device. Putting the tablet into kid mode doesn't touch your
phone.

A short notice appears every single time you start it, and you have to accept
it. Here's what it says, and we mean it plainly:

**Kid mode is a child lock, not a security system.** The device stays signed
in to your account. Kid mode hides your household's finances from view, and
the PIN stops a kid wandering out of it — but someone determined could get
around it. It's a screen you can hand to a nine-year-old, not a vault. Treat
the device the way you'd treat your unlocked phone.

## The kid button

Off until you turn it on. The switch is called **Let kids see some household
costs**, and it adds a small kid button to each transaction.

Mark one and your child sees what it was and what it cost — groceries, the
power bill, the vet. Money stops being a thing that happens invisibly to
adults.

- One item at a time, each with a confirmation. Nothing is shared in bulk.
- Children see only what you picked. Never the account, never the balance,
  never the rest of the list.
- Turn the switch back off and everything already shared disappears with it.

*→ [the kid button on a transaction](/screens/transactions/)*

## Archiving a kid

Kids grow up. Archiving one takes them off the page and stops the allowance —
and every payment already made stays in your household's books, where it
belongs. Nothing is rewritten backward. Last year's spending happened.
