# The Categories screen

Categories are the vocabulary of your budget. This screen is where you edit
the dictionary.

You start with a sensible set. Make it yours. A budget in your own words is a
budget you'll keep.

## Adding a category

Top bar: pick an icon, type a name, choose a group, press **Add**.
The icon button opens the picker — search "coffee" or "gym" or "dog" and
click. No hunting for emoji to copy-paste.

## The icon picker

Click any category's icon — or the icon button next to the new-category
field — and the picker opens. Three tabs, one search box, four thousand
icons and change:

- **Native** — your device's own emoji. The hand-picked budgeting set sits
  on top in familiar shelves (Money, Home, Food…), with the full library
  below.
- **OpenMoji** — about 2,500 open-source icons in a hand-drawn line style.
- **Fluent** — about 1,600 Microsoft icons, soft and rounded.

Search works by name or by keyword: "ev", "pizza", "charging". Long lists
cap at 360 results; keep typing to narrow.

Pick one and it shows everywhere the category appears: budget rows, reports,
dropdowns, modals. And every icon serves from our own site — no CDN calls,
no tracking pixels riding along. Your dog icon is nobody's business.

Two footnotes:

- The **Emoji card** on the [Account screen](/screens/account/) can hide a
  set's tab for the whole household. Icons already chosen keep showing —
  the toggle shapes the picker, never the data.
- In plain-text spots, like the category dropdown on a transaction, an
  OpenMoji or Fluent icon shows as its closest native emoji. Expected, not
  a bug.

## Editing

Everything on a row is editable in place:

- **Icon** — click it, pick a new one.
- **Name** — click, type, Enter. History follows the category; renaming is
  free.
- **Group dropdown** — move a category to another group.
- **Archive** — retires a category you no longer budget. Its history stays in
  your reports; it just stops appearing in the grid. Archived categories live
  at the bottom, one click from **Restore**.

Archive is almost always the right call. It preserves the past. Nothing is
deleted; nothing is lost.

## Groups

Groups are shelves — they organize, they don't hold money.

- **Rename** a group by clicking its name.
- **+ Group** at the bottom adds a shelf.
- **Delete** removes an empty one. If the group still has active categories,
  the app asks you to move or archive them first — deleting a shelf never
  throws away what's on it.

## A note on size

Fewer categories beat more. If you can't decide which of two categories a
purchase belongs to, they're one category. Start broad. Split only when a
number keeps surprising you and you need to see inside it.
