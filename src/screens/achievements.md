# The Achievements screen

Every other screen keeps track of what's left to do. This one counts what
you've already pulled off.

Find it in the top nav. On a phone, tap **⋯ More**, then **Achievements**.

## Two halves

**Your household** is the shared side. Everyone in the budget sees the same
wins here, whoever did the work: your first bank connected, Ready to Assign
hit zero, three months running of that, a month with nothing overspent,
your bank and your budget agreeing.

**You** is yours. Categorizing your first transaction, then fifty of them.
Splitting a purchase. Adding a tag. Leaving a note. Renaming something.
Marking a transfer.

Long-term goals bring two more to the household side: **Dream Big** for
starting one, **The Long Haul** for finishing one. The second takes years.
That's the joke, and it isn't a joke. *→ [Goals](/screens/goals/)*

Two people in one budget see the same household half and different personal
halves. The personal ones are read from the activity log, so they count
what you did while signed in as you. Share one login and everything lands
under that one name.

## Locked doesn't mean hidden

An achievement you haven't earned still shows its name and exactly what it
takes. That's deliberate. Several of them are features people never find on
their own — splits, tags, notes, marking a transfer — and a plain list of
what the app can do beats stumbling onto it a year from now.

Read the locked ones as a menu, not a chore.

## The card on the Budget screen

Earn something new and a small card appears at the top of Budget, above the
month. Dismiss it and it stays dismissed. Nothing here sends an email,
nothing needs setting up, and there's nothing to turn on.

## Nothing is ever taken back

Earn it once and it's yours. A rough month doesn't un-earn anything. No
streaks, nothing counting down, nothing to protect. This screen only knows
how to unlock.

Budgeting apps are loud about what went wrong. This is the one page that
keeps a record of what went right.
