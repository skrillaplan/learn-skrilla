# A budget that shows its work

Every budget app shows you a big number. Ours shows the work.

Tap **Ready to Assign** and the banner opens up like arithmetic on paper:
money that came in, minus money you gave a job, minus overspending carried
from before. Three lines, plain words, and they always add up to the number
you tapped. Always.

> A number you can't explain is a number you can't trust.

That's the whole feature, and it's the whole philosophy. Money apps have
trained people to accept mystery numbers — a balance appears, you squint,
you shrug, you hope. We think the squint is the problem. Trust isn't built
by being right; it's built by being checkable.

## Where mystery numbers come from

The classic: you connect your bank, it faithfully back-fills months of
history, and your budget announces you're $23,000 in the red. Panic.

Nothing was wrong. The math was right — the data was incomplete. All that
old spending happened before you had a plan, so counting it *against* your
plan tells a true story about your past and a false one about your budget.

So every SkrillaPlan budget has a **start month**, right there in the math
panel. History before it stays visible in Transactions and Reports — it's
your history, keep it — but it doesn't create debt in a plan that didn't
exist yet.

> Your budget starts when you start. Your history is context, not a verdict.

## Why we build like this

"Count honest" isn't a slogan we put on the box; it's the test every
feature has to pass. A budget is a relationship between you and your own
numbers, and relationships die of exactly one thing: mystery. So the log
can't be edited. Transfers are surfaced, never silently hidden. And the
big number at the top will show you its work, every time you ask.

You wouldn't accept "trust me" from a bank teller. Don't accept it from
software.
