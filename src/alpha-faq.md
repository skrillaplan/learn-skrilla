# Alpha FAQ

Short answers, no hedging. Longer stories are linked where they exist.

## Is it really free?

Yes, for everyone, during the alpha — with at least 30 days' notice before
that changes. Complete the feedback program and your household is free for
life. [The welcome page](/alpha/) states the trade plainly.

## Is my bank login safe?

We never see it. You sign in to your bank inside Plaid — the same service
most finance apps use — and your password never touches SkrillaPlan. We
receive transactions, read-only. We can't move money.

## What happens to my data if I leave?

You take it with you. **Transactions → ⬇ Export CSV** exports everything,
any time, in a file any spreadsheet opens. Leaving is as easy as staying —
that's deliberate.

## Why Google sign-in only?

Because we never want to hold a password. Google handles sign-in; we store
one less secret, and you remember one less password. It also makes
household invites simple — sign in with the invited email and you land in
the right budget.

## Why is my Ready to Assign weird?

Tap it — the number shows its own math. Usually the culprit is bank
history from before your budget existed; your **start month** fixes that
in one pick. *→ [A budget that shows its work](/show-the-math/)*

## Who sees my data?

Your household — the people you invited — and nobody else. No trackers, no
ads, no selling, no session recording. The full list of things we refuse
to do is short reading and worth it: [What we don't do](/what-we-dont-do/).

## Something's broken. Where do I yell?

The 🐛 button, top of the app. Yelling welcome. Confusion counts as broken
during the alpha — see [Troubleshooting](/troubleshooting/) for the quick
checks first.
